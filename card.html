Bengal Tiger

Bengal tigers have the longest canine teeth of any living felid measuring approximately 4 inches in large individuals.

  • Scientific Name: Panthera tigris
  • Average Length: 290 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 10 - 20 years
  • Habitat: deciduous forests, mangroves

The Bengal tiger's coat is yellow to light orange, with stripes ranging from dark brown to black; the belly and the interior parts of the limbs are white, and the tail is orange with black rings. The white tiger is a recessive mutant of the Bengal tiger, which is reported in the wild from time to time in Assam, Bengal, Bihar.
